# Contributor Covenant Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

We are committed to providing a welcoming and inspiring community for all. We pledge that everyone participating in the COMET project and its community will be treated with respect and dignity, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We are committed to creating and fostering a culture that empowers its members to lead, take action, and make decisions.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment include:
- Using welcoming and inclusive language
- Being respectful of differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences
- Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback
- Focusing on what is best for the community
- Showing empathy towards other community members
- Demonstrating patience and understanding with newcomers
- Acknowledging and celebrating contributions

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:
- The use of sexualized language or imagery, and unwelcome sexual attention or advances
- Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks
- Public or private harassment
- Publishing others' private information, such as a physical or electronic address, without explicit permission
- Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting
- Deliberate intimidation, stalking, or following
- Unwelcome physical contact
- Sustained disruption of conversations or events

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Community leaders are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards of acceptable behavior and will take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any behavior that they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

Community leaders have the right and responsibility to:
- Remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that are not aligned to this Code of Conduct
- Ban temporarily or permanently any contributor for behaviors that they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all project spaces, including but not limited to:
- GitHub repositories (issues, pull requests, discussions)
- Email and communication lists
- Webinars, workshops, and online events
- Conferences and in-person meetings
- Social media when representing the project

This Code of Conduct also applies outside official project spaces when:
- A person is representing the project or community
- The behavior occurs in the context of community affairs
- The behavior has the potential to negatively affect the safety and well-being of community members

## Reporting a Violation

Violations of this Code of Conduct should be reported by email to conduct@comet.carbon. Include:
- Your contact information
- Description of the incident
- Who was involved
- When and where it occurred
- Any relevant links or documentation

**Confidentiality**: All reports will be reviewed and investigated promptly and confidentially. Reporters will not be retaliated against for reporting violations in good faith.

**Response timeline**: You can expect acknowledgment within 48 hours and an investigation update within one week.

## Investigation and Resolution

The enforcement team will:
1. Review the report and gather additional information if needed
2. Determine whether a violation occurred
3. Take appropriate action:
   - Warning or private conversation
   - Temporary or permanent removal from community spaces
   - Other remedial actions
4. Communicate the outcome to both parties (within confidentiality limits)

**Appeals**: If you believe an enforcement decision is unfair, you may appeal to the Project Owner within 7 days of the decision.

## FAQ

**Q: What if someone is rude but not explicitly violating the Code?**
A: We value respectful discourse. If you feel a comment is unnecessarily harsh, consider a gentle private reminder. If it continues, report it.

**Q: What about disagreements about technical decisions?**
A: Technical disagreements are healthy and expected. This CoC applies to *how* we disagree - keep it respectful and focused on the issue, not the person.

**Q: Can I be banned for one mistake?**
A: No. We believe in proportional responses and growth. A first offense typically results in a private conversation and opportunity to learn.

**Q: What if I'm not sure if something violates the CoC?**
A: When in doubt, err on the side of respectfulness. If unsure about your own words, ask. If witnessing something questionable, report it. We'd rather have an unnecessary report than ignore a violation.

**Q: Who decides what is a violation?**
A: The enforcement team (Owner + designated Maintainers) reviews reports and makes determinations in good faith using these guidelines.
